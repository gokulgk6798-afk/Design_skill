## Content Guidelines

Effective UX writing creates a seamless experience by helping users understand, navigate, and complete tasks with confidence. Every piece of content should be clear, concise, consistent, and action-oriented, ensuring users always know what is happening and what they should do next.

## Voice

Maintain a consistent brand voice that is clear, professional, approachable, and human-centered. Use simple language instead of technical jargon, focus on user goals rather than system processes, and write with clarity before creativity.

## Tone

Adapt the tone based on the user's situation while keeping the same overall voice. Celebrate successes with encouraging language, communicate information in a neutral and helpful manner, present warnings calmly, explain errors with empathy, and use welcoming language for empty states.

## Labels

Use labels that are short, meaningful, and easy to understand. Prefer nouns for input fields and verbs for actions. Keep terminology consistent across the product and avoid unnecessary abbreviations or ambiguous wording.

## Error Messages

Error messages should clearly explain what went wrong and guide users toward a solution. Use plain language, avoid blaming the user, provide actionable next steps, and never expose technical or system-level information in user-facing interfaces.

## Notifications

Notifications should keep users informed without interrupting their workflow. Display only meaningful updates, communicate outcomes clearly, keep messages brief, and ensure every notification provides value to the user.

## Empty States

Empty states should help users understand why no content is available and encourage them to take the next appropriate action. Replace blank screens with helpful guidance, explain the current state, and provide clear calls to action whenever possible.

## Writing Principles

Write in sentence case, use active voice, keep content concise, and prioritize readability. Maintain consistent terminology across all screens, avoid unnecessary complexity, and ensure every message helps users complete their tasks with confidence. Every word should have a purpose, reduce cognitive effort, and contribute to a consistent, intuitive user experience.
